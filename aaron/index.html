Sometimes people ask me for a bio:

Aaron Schumacher is a data scientist and software engineer for Deep Learning Analytics. He has taught with Python and R for General Assembly and the Metis data science bootcamp. Aaron has also worked with data at Booz Allen Hamilton, New York University, and the New York City Department of Education. He studied mathematics at the University of Wisconsin–Madison and teaching mathematics at Bard College. Aaron's career-best breakdancing result was making it to the semi-finals of the R16 Korea 2009 individual footwork battle.

Here are some things I've made:

  • rjstat: an R package to read and write JSON-stat
  • Popularity Contest: A little hack on the twitter API. Likely to break.
  • NYC Test Data: some analysis/visualization of available NYC public school test data
  • skribi.es: simple experimental micro-survey or sign-up interface.

  • naldaramjui.com: interface for questions from the Test Of Proficiency In Korean

  • subway usage: D3 calendar visualization of NYC subway usage
  • school data explorer: my first d3 project, loaded with data animation messiness
